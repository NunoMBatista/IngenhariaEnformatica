# ChatGPT on super rendering...

## 1. “Rendering pixels selectively to simulate 4K”

This sounds like techniques where **not all pixels are computed equally**, but the final image *looks* higher resolution.

Examples:
* **Foveated rendering** → high detail only where the eye is focused (common in VR)
* **Checkerboard rendering** → only half the pixels rendered per frame, reconstructed later
* **Upscaling techniques** → render at lower resolution, then upscale intelligently

Goal:
- Reduce computation while maintaining perceived 4K quality

---

## 2. Use of CIELAB (CIELAB color space)

CIELAB color space is important because it models **human perception of color differences**.

Why that matters:
* Humans are **more sensitive to luminance (lightness)** than chroma (color)
* You can:
  * Render **L (lightness)** at higher resolution
  * Render **a/b (color channels)** at lower resolution
* Then reconstruct → looks sharp to the eye

This is similar in spirit to:
* YCbCr chroma subsampling (used in video compression)
* Perceptual rendering tricks

---

## 3. Is this super-resolution?

Not exactly—but it overlaps.

### True super-resolution

Super-resolution imaging usually means:
* Reconstructing a **higher-resolution image from lower-resolution input**
* Often using:

  * Multiple frames
  * AI models (e.g., deep learning)
  * Detail hallucination

---

### What you’re describing is closer to:

A hybrid of:

* **Perceptual rendering**
* **Adaptive sampling**
* **Upscaling / reconstruction**

It *can* include super-resolution, but it doesn’t have to.

---

## 4. Related real-world techniques

What you’re describing is very similar to modern GPU tricks:

* DLSS
  → renders lower resolution, uses AI to upscale

* FidelityFX Super Resolution
  → spatial/temporal upscaling

* Temporal Anti-Aliasing
  → reuses past frames to reconstruct detail

---

## 5. Big picture

So the idea is:
> “Exploit how humans perceive images to avoid fully rendering 4K, while still making it *look* like 4K.”

Using:
* Perceptual color spaces (like CIELAB)
* Selective pixel computation
* Reconstruction/upscaling
